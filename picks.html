The Starter Stack: 5 purchases that survive the honeymoon phase

New-pup checklists have 47 items. Most of it is noise. Nosey sniffed it down to the five that still matter in month six — with the honest reason for each.

1

The boring one: insurance (do this first)

Cheapest it will ever be is today, and anything diagnosed before you buy is excluded forever. Sixty seconds, five questions, your match.

2

Food you don't have to think about

Fresh, portioned delivery (The Farmer's Dog, Ollie) costs more than kibble — and solves the #1 new-owner spiral: "am I feeding them right?" Portions matched to your dog's age and weight, delivered before you run out.

Honest note: kibble from a reputable brand is also fine. Fresh is a convenience-and-peace-of-mind buy, not a moral one. Transition any new food over 7 days or you'll learn about dog digestion the hard way.

3

For rescues: the DNA test

54% of adopted dogs are mixed — and a $130–200 kit (Embark) tells you what's actually in there, including breed-specific health risks worth watching for. Best done early, while everything is still insurable.

Honest note: results don't change your premium — insurers price what's diagnosed, not what's possible. The value is knowing which symptoms to catch early.

4

The gear, in one order

Crate, leash, harness, bowls, chew toys they'll destroy anyway: one Chewy order, done. Budget $200–400 for the first haul (it's in our cost calculator).

5

A vet in your pocket at 2 a.m.

Telehealth memberships (Pawp and similar) put a real vet on video chat for the "is this an emergency or did he just eat grass?" nights. Pairs with insurance, doesn't replace it.

Honest note: some insurers bundle virtual vet access free (Fetch does) — check your match before paying separately.
