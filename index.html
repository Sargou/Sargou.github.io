Things people ask Nosey

Is pet insurance actually worth it?

Simple test: would a surprise $4,000 surgery bill hurt? Roughly 1 in 3 pets needs emergency care each year, and a single ER visit averages $800–1,500. If you have deep savings, self-insuring can work. If not, insurance turns a disaster into a monthly bill you can plan for.

Why does everyone say "insure them young"?

Because nothing your pet is diagnosed with BEFORE you buy is ever covered — that's a "pre-existing condition," and it follows them to every company, forever. Insure a healthy young pet and everything that comes later is covered. Wait a year and that limp, allergy, or tooth problem is excluded for life.

I just adopted — the shelter gave me "30 days of free insurance." Now what?

Nice, gotcha day! 🎉 Those trial policies (usually activated through microchip registration) are real but short — and they lapse or convert to a pricier plan fast. The smart play: use week one to compare real quotes while your dog is healthy and claim-free, then enroll before day 30. Any gap in coverage means anything diagnosed in between becomes a pre-existing condition — excluded everywhere, forever. Two-thirds of young dog owners adopt, and this 30-day window is the single most common insurance mistake they make.
